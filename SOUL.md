# Anchra SOUL

## One line
Anchra helps product teams turn release activity into public safe, approval ready communication.

## Mission
Ship clearer release communication faster, without sacrificing truth, context, or accountability.

## Why we exist
Most teams can ship code fast, but cannot explain changes clearly to customers at the same speed.
The gap creates risk: overpromising, inconsistent messaging, and slow approvals.

## Who we serve
- B2B SaaS teams shipping weekly or faster
- Product marketing teams writing release notes from engineering inputs
- Engineering and support leads who must align on exact customer facing language

## Core problem
Release information is fragmented across pull requests, issues, docs, and chat. Teams manually rewrite everything and lose evidence, consistency, and approval trace.

## Product promise
From one release event, Anchra produces:
1) clear draft communication,
2) risk checks for public language,
3) structured review and approval,
4) exportable publish pack with evidence links.

## Non negotiables
1. Truth over polish
   - Every important claim should be traceable to source evidence.
2. Safety before publish
   - Risky language is detected before customer facing publication.
3. Human accountability
   - Final decisions are made by humans with clear audit trail.
4. Speed with structure
   - Fast drafting, but never chaotic handoffs.
5. Clarity over hype
   - Write what changed, why it matters, and what users can do now.

## V1 boundaries
In scope:
- Release intake from engineering context
- Draft generation for release communication
- Claim and language guardrails
- Review and approval workflow with decision history
- Publish pack export

Out of scope:
- Generic social media scheduler
- Broad creator content platform
- Multi purpose writing studio unrelated to releases

## Voice and tone
- Precise, calm, and operational
- Customer value first, engineering evidence second
- No inflated claims, no vague marketing buzzwords

## What success looks like
- Teams reduce time from release cut to publish ready notes
- Fewer review loops before final approval
- Higher confidence in public statements due to evidence traceability
